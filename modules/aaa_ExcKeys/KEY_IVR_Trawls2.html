Deep-Sea Trawling II

  1. The indicator variables are defined below. Note that the “ANR” species will be the reference group
    • “C” where C=1 if the species is “COA” and A=0 if the species is not “COA”.
    • “S” where S=1 if the species is “SYK” and S=0 if the species is not “SYK”.
Note:
  • The indicator variables are named after the non-reference group. The reference group is alphabetically first in R. Thus, in this case the reference groups is “ANR” and the indicator variables are named after “COA” and “SYK.”


  1. The ultimate full model is μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH + δ1C + δ2S + γ1C×DEPTH + γ2S×DEPTH.
Note:
  • The variables are entered into the ultimate full model in this order – covariate (i.e., depth), indicator variable (i.e., C and S), and the interaction variable.
  • The covariate is always multiplied by β, the indicator variables are always multiplied by δs, and the interaction variables are always multiplied by γs.


  1. The submodels for both groups are below
    • ANR: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH
    • COA: μLOG.MASS = (α+δ1) + (β+γ1)DEPTH
    • SYK: μLOG.MASS = (α+δ2) + (β+γ2)DEPTH
Note:
  • The “ANR” submodel is found by plugging 0 in for C and S in the ultimate full model and then simplifying. Essentially everything in the ultimate full model multiplied by C or S is dropped.
  • The “COA” submodel is found by plugging 1 in for C and 0 for S in the ultimate full model and then simplifying.
  • The “SYK” submodel is found by plugging 0 in for C and 1 for S in the ultimate full model and then simplifying.


  1. Interpretations of the coefficients are below.
    • α is the intercept for ANR (reference group).
    • β is the slope for ANR (reference group).
    • δ1 is the difference in intercept of COA and ANR (i.e., COA-ANR)
    • δ2 is the difference in intercept of SYK and ANR (i.e., SYK-ANR)
    • γ1 is the difference in slopes of COA and ANR (i.e., COA-ANR)
    • γ2 is the difference in slopes of SYK and ANR (i.e., SYK-ANR)


  1. The models for the “parallel lines test” are below.
    • H0: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH + δ1C + δ2S
    • HA: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH + δ1C + δ2S + γ1C×TIME + γ2S×TIME
Note:
  • The models in a “parallel lines” test differ only in whether the γ parameters or the interaction variable exist as these are the parameters/variables that indicate if the slopes differ (non-parallel) or not (parallel). Thus, the HA is always the ultimate full model and the H0 is always the ultimate full model without the interaction variables.


  1. The models for the “intercepts test” (assuming parallel lines) are below.
    • H0: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH
    • HA: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH + δ1C + δ2S
Note:
  • The “equal intercepts” or “coincident lines” test is only tested assuming that the lines are parallel; thus, neither model will contain the γ parameters or the interaction variables (as γ1=0 and γ2=0 for parallel lines). Thus, the models in the “coincident lines” test differ only in whether the models contain the δ parameters or the indicator variables.
