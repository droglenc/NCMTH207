Deep-Sea Trawling

  1. The indicator variable is named “SCAV” where SCAV=1 if the species is a “scavenger” and SCAV=0 if the species is a “non-scavenger” (the reference group).
Note:
  • The indicator variable is named after the non-reference group. The reference group is alphabetically first in R. Thus, in this case the reference groups is “non-scavengers” and the indicator variable is named after “scavengers.”
  1. The ultimate full model is μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH + δ1SCAV + γ1SCAV×DEPTH.
Note:
  • The variables are entered into the ultimate full model in this order – covariate (i.e., depth), indicator variable (i.e., SCAV), and the interaction variable.
  • The covariate is always multiplied by β, the indicator variable(s) is/are always multiplied by δs, and the interaction variable(s) is/are always multiplied by γs.
  1. The submodels for both groups are below
    • non-scavenger: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH
    • scavenger: μLOG.MASS = (α+δ1) + (β+γ1)DEPTH
Note:
  • The “non-scavengers” submodel is found by plugging 0 in for SCAV in the ultimate full model and then simplifying. Essentially everything in the ultimate full model multiplied by SCAV is dropped.
  • The “scavengers” submodel is found by plugging 1 in for SCAV in the ultimate full model and then simplifying. Essentially the word SCAV is dropped, the two constants (α and δ1) are lumped together, and the two items multiplied by the covariate (β and γ1) are lumped together.
  1. Interpretations of the coefficients are below.
    • α is the intercept of non-scavengers (reference group).
    • β is the slope of non-scavenger (reference group).
    • δ1 is the difference in intercept of scavenger and non-scavenger groups (i.e., scavenger-nonscavenger)
    • γ1 is the difference in slopes of scavenger and non-scavenger groups (i.e., scavenger-nonscavenger)
  1. The models for the “parallel lines test” are below.
    • H0: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH + δ1SCAV
    • HA: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH + δ1SCAV + γ1SCAV×TIME
Note:
  • The models in a “parallel lines” test differ only in whether the γ1 parameter or the interaction variable exists as this is the parameter/variable that indicates if the slopes differ (non-parallel) or not (parallel). Thus, the HA is always the ultimate full model and the H0 is always the ultimate full model without the interaction variables.
  1. The models for the “intercepts test” (assuming parallel lines) are below.
    • H0: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH
    • HA: μLOG.MASS = α + βDEPTH + δ1SCAV
Note:
  • The “equal intercepts” or “coincident lines” test is only tested assuming that the lines are parallel; thus, neither model will contain the γ1 parameter or the interaction variable (as γ1=0 for parallel lines). Thus, the models in the “coincident lines” test differ only in whether the models contain the δ1 parameter or the indicator variable.
  1. The data for the scavengers appears curved, which violates the assumption of linearity.
