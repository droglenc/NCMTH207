Male-Female Birth Ratio

  1. The dfResidual=n-2, thus n is dfResidual+2. In this case dfResidual=19 so n is 21.
  2. The variance of the proportion of males among years ignoring any relationship with year is MSTotal=0.00000018(=\(\frac{0.00000356}{20}\)).
  3. The variance of the proportion of males among years after taking into account the relationship between proportion of males and year is MSResidual=0.00000007.
  4. There is a significant relationship between the proportions of males and years (p=0.000014).
  5. The p-values here (p=0.000014) and from the slope test on the previous exercise (p=0.000014) are the same. This occurs because the two tests effectively test the same null and alternative hypothesis. Furthermore, an F with 1 an 19 df is equal to the square of a t with 19 df.

Willow Flycatcher Migration

  1. The dfTotal=n-1, thus n is dfTotal+1. In this case dfTotal=21 so n is 22.
  2. There is a significant relationship between wing length and day of migration (p=0.0444).
  3. The F-ratio here (=4.5995) is the square of t test statistic for the slope from the previous exercise (=-2.14462=4.5995). This occurs because the two tests effectively test the same null and alternative hypothesis. Furthermore, an F with 1 an 20 df is equal to the square of a t with 20 df.
  4. The percent of the variability in wing length is explained by knowing the day of migration is 18.7 (i.e., 100r2).
  5. The variance of wing length among birds after taking into account day of migration is MSResidual=2.805.
  6. The variance of wing length among birds ignoring any relationship with day of migration is MSTotal=3.286(=\(\frac{69.008}{21}\)).

Car Drive Ratio and Gas Mileage

  1. There is not a significant relationship between gas mileage and drive ratio for imported cars (p=0.3485).
  2. The percent of the variability in gas mileage explained by knowing the drive ratio of an imported car is 4.9. Note that \(r^{2}\)=\(\frac{\text{SS}_{\text{Regression}}}{\text{SS}_{\text{Total}}}\)=\(\frac{37.309}{762.165}\)=0.049.
  3. The variance of gas mileage among cars ignoring any relationship with drive ratio is MSTotal=40.114(=\(\frac{762.165}{19}\)).
  4. The variance of gas mileage among cars after taking into account drive ratio is MSResidual=40.270.
  5. The plot is shown below.
