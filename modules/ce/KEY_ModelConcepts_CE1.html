Calculations

  1. The group means are ȳA=16 and ȳB=25.
  2. The grand mean is ȳ=21.
  3. The residual from the grand mean for the first individual is 13-21=-8. The residuals from the grand mean for all individuals are – -8, -7, -4, -1, 0, 2, 5, 6, 7.
  4. The residual from the grand mean for the first individual in group A tells us that that individual is 8 units below average relative to all individuals in the data set, regardless of which group they belong to.
  5. The residual from the grand mean for the last individual in group B tells us that that individual is 7 units above average relative to all individuals in the data set, regardless of which group they belong to.
  6. The RSS for the grand mean model is 244.
  7. The residual df for the grand mean model is 8.
  8. The residual MS for the grand mean model is \(\frac{244}{8}\)=30.50. The square root of this value is approximately equal to the mean of the absolute values of the residuals (from the grand mean).
  9. The square root of this residual MS (i.e., 5.52) measures the natural variability of individuals around the grand mean. It is the “noise” around the grand mean.
  10. The residual from the group mean for the first individual is 13-16=-3. The residuals from the grouop means for all individuals are – -3, -2, 1, 4, -4, -2, 1, 2, 3.
  11. The residual from the group mean for the first individual in group A tells us that that individual is 3 units below average relative to all individuals in group A.
  12. The residual from the group mean for the last individual in group B tells us that that individual is 3 units above average relative to all individuals in group B.
  13. The RSS for the group means model is 64.
  14. The residual df for the group means model is 7.
  15. The residual MS for the group means model is \(\frac{64}{7}\)=9.14. The square root of this value is approximately equal to the mean of the absolute values of the residuals (from the observation’s group mean).
  16. The square root of this residual MS (i.e., 3.02) measures the pooled natural variability of individuals around their respective group means. It is the “noise” around the group means.
  17. The RSSTotal-RSSWithin is 244-64=180. This value is the reduction in RSS from using the group means rather than the grand means. It is the improvement in “lack-of-fit” from using the groupp means rather than the grand mean or how much better the group means model fits compared to the grand mean model.
  18. The dfTotal-dfWithin is 8-7=1. This value is the difference in number of parameters between the group means and grand mean models. It is how much more complex the group means model is than the grand mean model.
